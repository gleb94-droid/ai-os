# 🖨️ Sfalim print methods & how to talk about them

> So content captions + customer messages are **accurate AND plain**. Read before writing any product copy for [[sfalimshop]] / [[glebalife]].

## The methods (what goes on what)
- **DTF (Direct-to-Film)** → **full-colour designs / photo collages** on fabric, incl. **dark shirts** (has a white underbase so colours show on black too). **This is what the MY CREW / photo-collage tees use** (e.g. Rafael's "ONLY CHUCKY" collage — **XL black + M white are DTF, NOT DTV**). Application: pre-press garment 5-10s → press transfer **~150-160°C / ~15s / medium-high pressure** → **COLD peel** (wait until fully cool, most DTF) → **post-press** 10-15s under parchment/teflon for durability. Don't mirror. ⚠️ *cold peel — opposite of DTV.*
- **Glitter HTV (heat-transfer vinyl)** → **solid-colour cut** designs for **fabric / clothing** (shirts, bags, hats). Design is **cut on the Cricut then heat-pressed**. **NOT for mugs.** Glitter colours from **craftisrael.co.il** (42-colour catalog).
- **Siser EasyColor DTV** → printable HTV, works on **dark shirts** (press ~155°C / 15s / medium / **hot peel**, don't mirror).
- **Sublimation (Epson printer + heat press)** → for **mugs + coasters** (hardboard coaster ~195–200°C / 60s) and other coated blanks. Genuinely "printing."
- Quick rule: **full-colour photo/collage designs = DTF · solid-colour cut designs = HTV/DTV (Cricut) · mugs/coasters/ceramics = sublimation.**
- ⚠️ **Don't confuse DTF (full-colour film transfer, cold peel) with DTV (printable cut HTV, hot peel).**

## ⚠️ Copy rule — plain language, no trade jargon (Gleb, 2026-07-01)
- **Don't use technical words customers don't get** — e.g. never "I cut on shirts / חותך על חולצות." People don't know what that means.
- **Say it plainly:** *"you pick a colour and I turn it into a design on your shirt"* (*אתה בוחר צבע ואני הופך אותו לעיצוב על החולצה*).
- **Explain what makes the material special in simple terms** — glitter = *נוצץ ותופס אור בולט הרבה יותר מהדפס רגיל* (sparkles, catches light, stands out far more than a regular print).
- Keep it **low-key, no pafos** (ties to [[glebalife-content]] voice + the low-key message rule).
